Meet Azusa.

Azusa needs a tool to track and make her aware of her information consumption habits.

She's a journalism student aspiring to be the next Bob Woodward.

She really likes politics, and is trying better inform herself about relevant political issues, figures, so she can someday work for The Post.

She knows that she spends too much time on Facebook and reading TMZ, and she wants to develop better reading habits outside of her academic curriculum.

She’s looking for a tool to make her more aware of the content she is reading, and to help change her habits to consume better information.

Azusa then downloads the browser extension, Slimformation.

Slimformation

Use Case

Once Slimformation is installed onto her browser, it begins to track how long Azusa has a certain web page open as her active tab.

After a week of using Slimformation, Azusa clicks on the Slimformation icon in her browser toolbar, which displays her information intake split up by category.

The Dashboard

Use Case, ctd

She realizes she’s reading way more TMZ than politics.

Azusa then becomes more conscious about the material she reads online – and makes a concerted effort to follow politics more closely.

After a month passes, Azusa clicks on the Slimformation icon again and notices her information intake graph now is dominated by the politics category and tabloids only represent a small sliver.
